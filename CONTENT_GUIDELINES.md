### Why Article Images Matter
Having cover images for articles significantly improves user engagement and the overall reading experience:

**‚úÖ Benefits of Having Article Images:**
- **Better Visual Appeal**: Images make your article list more attractive and professional
- **Higher Click Rates**: Articles with images get 94% more views than those without

### Recommendation: **ALWAYS ADD IMAGES** ‚ú®
We **strongly recommend** adding cover images to all articles. Even a simple, relevant image is better than no image at all.

### Where to Find Free, High-Quality Images:
1. **Unsplash** (https://unsplash.com) - Professional photos, completely free
2. **Pexels** (https://www.pexels.com) - High-quality stock photos
3. **Pixabay** (https://pixabay.com) - Free images and illustrations
4. **Canva** (https://www.canva.com) - Create custom graphics

### Image Specifications:
- **Recommended Size**: 1200 x 630 pixels (ideal for social sharing)
- **Minimum Size**: 800 x 400 pixels
- **Format**: JPG or PNG
- **File Size**: Keep under 500 KB for fast loading
- **Aspect Ratio**: 16:9 or 2:1 works best

## üìù Article Content - Uniqueness & Quality

### The Importance of Unique Content

Your article content should be:
- **Specific to the Topic**: Every word should relate directly to the article title
- **Unique and Original**: Avoid generic or copied content
- **Well-Structured**: Use clear headings, paragraphs, and formatting
- **Valuable**: Provide real insights, tips, or information

### Content Quality Checklist:

#### ‚úÖ Good Article Content:
- **Matches the Title**: Content directly addresses what the title promises
- **Keyword-Rich**: Uses relevant terms naturally throughout
- **Well-Organized**: Clear introduction, body, and conclusion
- **Specific Examples**: Real-world examples and case studies
- **Unique Insights**: Your own perspective or research
- **Proper Length**: Adequate depth (typically 800+ words for quality)
- **Engaging**: Keeps readers interested from start to finish

#### ‚ùå Avoid:
- **Generic Text**: "Lorem ipsum" or placeholder text
- **Copied Content**: Plagiarized from other sources
- **Off-Topic**: Content that doesn't match the title
- **Too Short**: Articles under 300 words seem incomplete
- **Duplicate Words**: Repeating the same phrases unnecessarily
- **Keyword Stuffing**: Forcing keywords unnaturally

### Tips for Writing Unique Article Content:

1. **Start with Research**
   - Research your topic thoroughly
   - Find multiple sources
   - Take notes on unique angles

2. **Create an Outline**
   - Plan your structure before writing
   - Organize main points logically
   - Ensure flow between sections

3. **Write in Your Voice**
   - Use your natural writing style
   - Add personal insights or experiences
   - Make it conversational but professional

4. **Use Specific Examples**
   - Include real-world scenarios
   - Add statistics or data
   - Reference credible sources

5. **Edit and Refine**
   - Remove redundant phrases
   - Check for clarity
   - Ensure every sentence adds value

### Content Structure Template:

```markdown
# [Engaging Title]

## Introduction (150-200 words)
- Hook the reader
- State the problem/topic
- Preview what they'll learn

## Section 1: [Main Point]
- Detailed explanation
- Examples
- Supporting evidence

## Section 2: [Second Point]
- Build on previous section
- More specific details
- Practical applications

## Section 3: [Additional Insights]
- Advanced tips
- Common mistakes to avoid
- Best practices

## Conclusion (100-150 words)
- Summarize key points
- Call to action
- Final thoughts
```

## üéØ Category & Tag Guidelines

### Choosing the Right Category:
- Select the **most relevant** category for your article
- Only use one primary category per article
- Categories help users find related content quickly

### Using Tags Effectively:
- Add 3-5 relevant tags per article
- Use specific terms, not generic ones
- Tags should represent key topics covered
- Examples:
  - Good: "React Hooks", "State Management", "Performance"
  - Bad: "Technology", "Programming", "Coding"

## üìä Reading Time Estimation

The system automatically calculates reading time based on:
- **Average Reading Speed**: 200 words per minute
- **Content Length**: Total word count
- **Formula**: `Reading Time = Word Count √∑ 200`

To optimize reading time display:
- Aim for 800-2000 words for standard articles
- Break longer content into series
- Use subheadings to make longer articles scannable
